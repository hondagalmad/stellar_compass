Pluto

Basic Stats


Type: Dwarf Planet

Diameter: ~2,377 km

Mass: ~0.0022× Earth’s mass

Moons: 5 known moons (largest: Charon)

Length of day: ≈ 153 hours

Length of year: ≈ 248 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~5.9 billion km

Average temperature: ≈ −229°C

Composition: Rock and ice

Atmosphere & Surface


Pluto has a thin, icy atmosphere mainly of nitrogen, with traces of methane and carbon monoxide. Its surface is covered with ice and rocky regions, and it has large plains and mountains. Pluto's atmosphere expands when it is closer to the Sun and freezes onto the surface when it moves farther away.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

The only spacecraft to visit Pluto is NASA’s New Horizons, which flew by in 2015. It captured detailed images, studied its atmosphere, moons, and surface features.

Upcoming Missions

Currently, no confirmed missions are planned for Pluto, but astronomers continue to study it with telescopes and hope for future exploration.

Weird Facts

  • Pluto was considered the ninth planet until reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.
  • Its orbit is highly elliptical and sometimes closer to the Sun than Neptune.
  • Pluto has five moons, the largest being Charon, which is almost half its size.
  • Its atmosphere is thin and can freeze onto the surface.
  • Pluto has large icy plains and mountains made of water ice.
  • Surface temperatures are extremely cold, around −229°C.
  • Pluto’s day lasts about 6.4 Earth days.
