Uranus

Basic Stats


Type: Ice Giant

Diameter: ~50,724 km

Mass: ~14.5× Earth’s mass

Moons: 27 known moons

Length of day: ≈ 17.2 hours

Length of year: ≈ 84 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~2.9 billion km

Average temperature: ≈ −224°C

Composition: Hydrogen, helium, and icy materials (water, methane, ammonia)

Atmosphere & Surface


Uranus has a cold, icy atmosphere rich in hydrogen, helium, and methane. Its methane gas absorbs red light, giving the planet its blue-green color. The planet has no solid surface—its “surface” is a swirling icy atmosphere above a fluid interior. Uranus is also tilted at an extreme 98°, causing unusual seasons that last over 20 years each.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

Only one spacecraft has ever visited Uranus: NASA’s Voyager 2 in 1986. It flew past the planet, capturing images, studying its atmosphere, discovering new moons, and investigating its rings and magnetic field.

Upcoming Missions

Astronomers and NASA have proposed a “Uranus Orbiter and Probe” mission to study the planet’s atmosphere, rings, moons, and mysterious magnetic field. If approved, it may launch in the 2030s and arrive around the 2040s.

Weird Facts

  • Uranus rotates on its side with a tilt of 98°—like it’s rolling around the Sun.
  • It has the coldest atmosphere in the Solar System, even colder than Neptune.
  • The planet has faint rings made of dark particles.
  • Its magnetic field is extremely tilted and off-center compared to the planet.
  • Methane in its atmosphere gives Uranus its blue-green color.
  • Winds on Uranus can reach 900 km/h.
  • Each pole experiences 42 years of sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness.
